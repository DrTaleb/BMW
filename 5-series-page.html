The history of BMW 5 series

BMW E12 (1972)

The first BMW 5 Series design signature was created in 1972. The BMW e12 can be considered the father of today's perfect BMW 5 Series. Its design was presented in the style of cars of the seventies with a more sporty appearance. With dual grills and rounded lights that will never be erased from the mind of any BMW lover.In later years, models with more powerful engines such as the 528 (165 hp) and 525 (145 hp) were introduced.Facelift models of this car were introduced in 1977, and models such as the 525 and 528 were equipped with dual carburetors, which increased their acceleration and power. Finally, with the production of less than 700,000 units, the production of e12 was stopped and replaced by the next model

BMW E28 (1981)

The new generation 3 Series had many changes in engine technology and occupant safety, despite minor design changes.These changes included a catalytic converter, a manifold injection system, an electric motor control system and abs brakes, and airbags for the occupants.BMW's unparalleled advances in diesel engines led to the installation of the first diesel engines in the series on BMW 5 Series with this chassis. The fastest diesel car in the world at the time was the BMW 524td (115 hp).
The BMW M535i was a reflection of BMW 5 series progress. The 6-cylinder engine with 218 hp at that time made this car very popular.
And it was in 1985 that a legend called the m5 was produced. Which proved to the world that we do not need to pay for a super sports car to experience a fast and enjoyable ride.
In the end of story BMW has produced 772000 vehicles from the BMW 5 series (E28)

BMW E34 (1988)

After the introduction of the E34 in January 1988, BMW entered a new phase in design and aerodynamics.َAccording to almost optimum weight distribution of 50:50 on the front and rear axles And the long wheelbase.The BMW E34 was the first generation 5 Series to reach 200 km / h

The first BMW 5 Series 4-wheel drive car in this generation was introduced with the name 525ix.

Also, the first 5 Series cars in this generation were equipped with V8 engines in 530i and 540i models
BMW m5 was produced in this generation with a 3.8-liter 6-cylinder engine with 350hp

BMW E39 (1995)

The new generation BMW 5 Series, a larger combination with the same weight as the previous generation. How? Thanks to the use of aluminum in the suspension of this generation.Increased torsional strength, high drag coefficient, occupant safety and High resistance to rust thanks to the use of hot-dip galvanizing in three-quarters of the body made this car the best in its class

And in 1998, a new technology makes its debut in the diesel engines: with the first inline 6-cylinder common rail power unit, BMW sets global standards in terms of the performance and efficiency of diesel engines that are to last for years. The modern engines inspire with their performance and smooth running, from the 4-cylinder diesel to the exceptionally strong V8-cylinder power unit with 294 kW (400 hp) in the BMW M5.

This generation of BMW 5 Series broke a new record by selling 1.48 million cars, 266 thousand of which are Turing models.

BMW E60 (2003)

With the introduction of this generation, BMW entered a new phase of design. More flexibility and arches in the body, up-to-date design of headlights and amenities were very much a sign of the progress of this generation. Unfortunately, today, due to the technical costs and short life of the engine and its sensitivities, it has reduced its popularity compared to previous generations.

In terms of size, this generation is more spacious and larger than its predecessor, and its weight has been reduced by about 75 kg due to the use of more aluminum in the front part.

Up-to-date features of this car include head-up display, adaptive rotation and braking Dynamic Drive Roll Stabilizer and Active Cruise Control (ACC). Combined with sports suspension lights is optional.
Equipped in 2005 with the M5 powered by a 5-liter 10-cylinder engine, this generation of the M5 became one of the fastest estate cars in the world.

BMW G30 (2017)

Undoubtedly, one of the most beautiful designs of BMWs in the current generation is the g30 design. Less arches and more angles can be seen in its design compared to f10. The headlights were artistically combined with the grille to create a beautiful effect.The moderation in the design of this model is quite evident and it does not mention the rabbit tooth grill and the strange designs of today's BMWs.
In 2020, the facelift of this model entered the market with narrower headlights, different grille and more angular design.
In addition to beautiful design and unrivaled engines, advanced auxiliary systems such as lane keeping system and emergency braking, etc. make driving safe and comfortable.
