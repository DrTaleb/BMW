The history of BMW 7 series

The first generation E23 (1977)

A perfect luxury generation from BMW started with the production of the E23 in 1977. In terms of the design of other BMW cars, it had the signature of BMW in its grill. This design by Paul Bracq introduced a perfect example of a perfect Bavarian design, a new symbol of a luxury car.
Among the modern electronic systems of this car, we can mention ABS brakes and electronic climate control system, as well as digital engine electronics and injection system.
In 1980, the BMW 745i was produced with a power of 252 horsepower and a maximum speed of 227 km/h, and in 1982, an updated model with a modified chassis, better aerodynamics and a four-speed automatic transmission was released.
This generation was the beginning of one of the most successful BMW series in the future, with more than 270,000 vehicles produced for more than 9 years.

The second generation E32 (1986)

Regarding the design of this generation, we should say that it has used a similar design pattern with models like E34. The twin lights and grill inherited from the previous generation, but with more aerodynamics and elongation in the design
From 1987, the high-end sedan was produced with a 12-cylinder engine with a power of 300 horsepower, and this was the first engine of its kind to be used in German automobile manufacturing. From 1989, optional equipment such as refrigerator, mobile phone, etc. were added to this model, which brought luxury cars to a new level.
In 1991, for the first time in this generation, xenon lighting was used as an option in a production car
And two 3- and 4-liter engines were also used for this car, and a total of 310,000 cars were produced in this generation.

The third generation E38 (1994)

In 1994, E38 was introduced with its nineties design. In terms of design, it had more arches and more elongation than the previous generation.
In terms of technology, the 7 series in this generation has many innovations, including the first navigation system in European cars and a side airbag, and there are many luxury features that were rare and perhaps unique in their time.
The first 7 series model to enter the market with a diesel engine. including a six-cylinder engine with a power of 184 horsepower, a six-cylinder engine with a lower power of 143 horsepower and a V8 diesel engine with a power of 245 horsepower, which was the most powerful diesel engine.
The variety of engines in this model is not only limited to diesel engines, 750i, 750iL with a 5.4-liter 12-cylinder gasoline engine with a power of 326 horsepower is a symbol of the power of the e38 among competitors. And since 2000, the technology of hydrogen engines with the production of 750 hL enters the 7 series.
In addition to the produced models of the 7 series, about 900 units were produced with a 39 cm longer chassis called the L7.
A total of 327,000 BMW e38s were produced.

The fourth generation E65, E66, E67, E68 (2001)

The new century and the beginning of the new generation of the 7 series, designed by Chris Bangle's team.
The extraordinary interior design of this model is one of the most beautiful and impressive interior designs of the BMW 7 Series. The combination of wood and leather along with new electronic systems and the use of a mouse instead of many buttons promised the beginning of the new generations of today's 7 series.
Changing the location of the monitor from under the air conditioning vents to above them was done in this generation and continues in the next generations
The attractiveness of this car does not end only with its interior design, the 6-speed automatic gearbox with gear selector from behind the steering wheel is one of the other innovations of this generation.
The 12-cylinder, six-liter engine of this car produces 445 horsepower
Features of other models:

  • E66 : more distance of 14 cm between the rear and front axles
  • E67 : a model with high security version
  • E68 :hydrogen-powered version

The fifth generation F01, F02 (2008)

BMW F01 is a symbol of the acceptable trend in the development of the design of this series.
Safety and auxiliary systems in this car, such as head-up, 360-degree cameras, symbolize the entry and dominance of technology in the car world.
Gasoline engines v12 twin turbo in BMW 760i with power of 544 horsepower and diesel engines show sports performance. And for the first time, a hybrid drive is available in the BMW 7 series. And besides these, Electronic Damper Control (EDC) helps to drive as confidently as possible.
In 2012, the new update of this generation has more efficient engines, redesigned auxiliary systems and new central display with 3D visuals.

The sixth generation G11, G12 (2015)

The seventh heaven of BMW reached the peak of its beauty and glory in this generation.
The smooth lines that continue throughout the length of the car and all the parts in this car are proportional and eye-catching.
The use of aluminum and steel in the car body along with CFRP (carbon fiber reinforced plastic) has made the car lighter.
The interior design of the G11 is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful interior designs in the world. Linear lights that continue throughout the interior of the car and change their color when the driving mode is changed. In the front parts, the steering wheel and the seats are made of leather in attractive colors. The digital instrument panel changes shape and color by changing the driving mode.

Exclusive, highly crafted materials express your personal preferences – for instance Nappa leather upholstery with “Cognac” quilting, complementary interior trims and the roof lining. The rear passenger entertainment system is equipped with HD touch screens. With the Executive Lounge option, rear passengers enjoy maximum comfort thanks to the luxurious seats with adjustable massage functions. Occupants sitting behind the front passenger have an unobstructed panoramic view, and their footrest is attached to the front passenger seat.
Because of its integrated SIM card, the BMW Personal Assistant has unlimited mobile access to apps and services, such as the continuously updated Connected Navigation, and remote software upgrades. BMW Connected represents the seamless connection between smartphone and vehicle. Semi-automated parking gives the driver control of the vehicle without being in it: The BMW 7 series can maneuver itself into parking spaces and garages with driver supervision.
With the model update in 2019 the car was given a new, significantly larger radiator grille, which, together with the matching headlights emphasize the width and presence of the luxury sedan.

The seventh generation G70 (2022)

BMW unveiled the seventh generation of the 7 Series in 2022. There were many doubts about its design before its unveiling. But after the unveiling, it was able to attract the positive opinion of customers.
In this generation, the headlights have become two separate parts below each other, and the upper part of the car's daytime running lights is made up of a narrow line of light.The customized model of headlights is a special type called 'Iconic Glow' which contains backlit Swarovski elements.
There are touch screens throughout the interior of this car, and the optional 31.3'' BMW Theater Screen creates a cinematic atmosphere for the rear passengers. The combination of this space and the crystal-clear sound and 4D audio of the optional Bowers & Wilkins Diamond Surround Sound System provides more pleasure for the rear passengers.
In this generation, hybrid and fully electric engines are produced for the first time in this generation. BMW, like all major car manufacturers in the world, is changing its engines from gasoline to hybrid and all-electric. The i7 is the first all-electric BMW seven-series with a power of 544 horsepower and a range of 600 kilometers, which is a symbol of BMW's powerful entry into the world of electric cars.
